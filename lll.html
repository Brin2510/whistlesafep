Know Your Rights: Empowering Every Indian Citizen

Your guide to fundamental laws and constitutional principles of India.

Fundamental Rights (Part III of the Constitution)

These are the cornerstone of Indian democracy, guaranteeing basic human rights to all citizens, enforceable by law.

Article 14: Equality before Law - Ensures that all citizens are treated equally under the law.
Article 19: Protection of certain rights regarding freedom of speech, etc. - Guarantees freedoms of speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, and profession (with reasonable restrictions).
Article 21: Protection of life and personal liberty - Protects the right to life and personal liberty, interpreted to include various aspects like privacy, health, and a clean environment.
Article 21A: Right to Education - Mandates free and compulsory education for children between 6 and 14 years of age.
Article 25: Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice and propagation of religion - Guarantees the freedom to practice and propagate one's religion.

Key Criminal Laws (Indian Penal Code, 1860)

Understanding these basic criminal laws is crucial for personal safety and responsible citizenship.

Section 354: Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty - Addresses acts of sexual harassment and assault.
Section 379: Theft - Defines and penalizes the act of stealing property.
Section 499: Defamation - Deals with the act of harming someone's reputation through false statements.
Section 509: Word, gesture or act intended to insult the modesty of a woman - Punishes actions intended to insult a woman's modesty.

Other Essential Legislation

These laws impact daily life and empower citizens in various aspects.

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 - Protects consumer rights and provides mechanisms for grievance redressal.
The Right to Information Act, 2005 - Empowers citizens to access information held by public authorities, promoting transparency.
Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (and amendments) - Regulates road transport, traffic rules, and vehicle insurance.
The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 - Provides legal protection and remedies to women facing domestic violence.
